Core Warrior Principles

武士道

Traditional Aikido training develops character through four essential bushido principles that shape both technique and daily life.

義

Gi - Rectitude

Moral Courage

The ability to make ethical decisions under pressure. In training, this means choosing the harder path of growth over the easier path of comfort.

勇

Yu - Courage

Heroic Bravery

Physical and moral bravery. Training demands the courage to be thrown, to fail publicly, and to persist through discouragement.

仁

Jin - Benevolence

Compassion

Kindness balanced with strength. We train hard while maintaining responsibility for our partners' development and safety through disciplined practice.

礼

Rei - Respect

Courtesy & Manners

Proper behavior and acknowledgment of others. Every bow, every protocol serves to cultivate humility and awareness.

誠

Makoto - Honesty

Sincerity

Truthfulness in word and action. Training with complete effort, accepting feedback without defensiveness.

名

Meiyo - Honor

Personal Dignity

Living according to principles even when no one is watching. Training with integrity in every technique.

忠

Chugi - Loyalty

Devotion to Duty

Commitment to your training, your teachers, and your fellow students. Faithfulness to the art and its principles.

Bushido in Daily Training

These virtues aren't abstract concepts—they're practical guidelines that shape every moment of training. Each class is an opportunity to develop these qualities through disciplined practice, honest effort, and respect for the art's transformative power.
