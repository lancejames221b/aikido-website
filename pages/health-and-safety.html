Traditional Discipline

Training Requirements

Safety in budo emerges from discipline, not comfort. Traditional training demands proper technique, focused attention, and respect for your partner's wellbeing. These requirements form the foundation of effective martial practice.

What Training Demands of Students

Traditional budo requires dedication to proper form, mental focus, and responsibility for yourself and your training partners. These demands create the conditions necessary for authentic martial development.

正

Proper Technique First

You must prioritize correct technique over speed or power. Precision prevents injury and builds the foundation for advanced practice. Sloppy technique endangers both you and your partner.

心

Constant Awareness

Develop zanshin (残心) - maintaining awareness of yourself, your partner, and the training space at all times. Distraction creates danger. Focused attention protects everyone on the mat.

責

Partner Responsibility

Take complete responsibility for your partner's safety. Control your technique, monitor their condition, and stop immediately if something feels wrong. Their wellbeing depends on your discipline.

忍

Physical Conditioning

Maintain the physical condition necessary for safe practice. Flexibility, strength, and endurance prevent injury and allow you to train effectively without compromising others' safety.

謙

Honest Self-Assessment

Know your current limitations and train within them. Ego-driven overextension creates injury. Honest practice allows gradual, sustainable progress without endangering yourself or others.

礼

Traditional Respect

Follow dojo etiquette completely. Bow properly, maintain clean uniforms, and show respect to instructors and training partners. These forms create the environment necessary for serious training.

Student Obligations

Traditional training requires specific commitments from each student. These obligations ensure everyone can train safely and effectively.

Physical Preparation

Arrive physically prepared for training. This means adequate rest, proper nutrition, and sufficient flexibility to perform techniques safely. Your preparation affects your partner's safety.

Mental Focus

Leave outside concerns at the door. Training requires complete mental presence. Distracted minds create injuries. Develop the discipline to focus entirely on the practice at hand.

Technique Precision

Execute techniques exactly as taught. Improvisation before mastery creates danger. Follow instruction precisely until the movements become natural and safe through repetition.

Communication Discipline

Communicate clearly with training partners about your limitations and theirs. Ask questions when uncertain. Speak up immediately if something feels unsafe. Silence can create injury.

Consistent Practice

Attend regularly to maintain conditioning and technique familiarity. Sporadic attendance creates gaps in skill that endanger both you and your training partners. Consistency builds safety.

Dojo Maintenance

Help maintain the training space. Clean equipment, arrange mats properly, and keep the dojo in condition suitable for serious practice. The training environment reflects your commitment to the art.

Training Reality

Traditional martial arts training involves controlled risk. Techniques work because they can cause harm when improperly applied. Your discipline, attention, and technical precision are the only things standing between effective training and injury.

Non-Negotiable Standards

These requirements apply to every student, regardless of experience level or personal circumstances. They cannot be modified or compromised.

Physical Standards

  • Clean gi and proper uniform at all times
  • Basic physical conditioning for safe technique execution
  • Sufficient flexibility to perform basic movements
  • Immediate disclosure of injuries or physical limitations

Mental Standards

  • Complete attention during instruction and practice
  • Immediate response to instructor commands
  • Mental presence throughout entire class
  • Willingness to accept correction without argument

Technical Standards

  • Precise execution of techniques as demonstrated
  • Controlled application appropriate to partner's skill level
  • Immediate cessation when partner signals discomfort
  • Questions asked only during appropriate times

Behavioral Standards

  • Proper bowing and dojo etiquette without exception
  • Respectful treatment of all students and equipment
  • Punctual arrival and full participation until dismissed
  • Complete commitment to partner's safety over personal goals

Accept Training Requirements

Traditional budo demands discipline, respect, and complete commitment to proper technique. If you can meet these requirements, authentic martial training awaits.
